## **Pañcāṅga (पञ्चाङ्ग): The Integrated Corpus**

The **Pañcāṅga** refers to the five essential components of the Paninian system, which work together to derive every word in the Sanskrit language:

1. **Śiva Sūtras**: The phonemic arrangement revealed by Lord Śiva.
2. **Aṣṭādhyāyī**: The 4,000 core sutras of grammar.
3. **Dhātupāṭha**: The list of approximately 2,000 verbal roots.
4. **Gaṇapāṭha**: Groups of words that follow specific rules.
5. **Liṅgānuśāsana**: The rules governing the gender of words.
6. **Uṇādisūtras**: Rules for deriving irregular or specialized words.
