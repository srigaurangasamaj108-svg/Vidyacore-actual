## **Nyāya: The Lamp of All Sciences**

Nyāya is the science of right reasoning (**Tattva-jñāna**). It provides the formal methodology to attain truth and remove illusion.

1. **Ṣoḍaśa-padārthāḥ (ID #233)**: The sixteen categories of reality established by Sage Gautama.
2. **Pramāṇa-vyavasthā (ID #227)**: The four means of valid knowledge: **Pratyakṣa** (Perception), **Anumāna** (Inference), **Upamāna** (Comparison), and **Śabda** (Verbal Testimony).

## **Reasoning & Refutation**

* **Pañcāvayava-yuktiḥ (ID #228)**: The world-renowned 5-step syllogism: Pratijñā (Proposition), Hetu (Reason), Udāharaṇa (Example), Upanaya (Application), and Nigamana (Conclusion).
* **Vāda-darśanam (ID #230)**: The classification of debate into **Vāda** (Truth-seeking), **Jalpa** (Victory-seeking), and **Vitaṇḍā** (Destructive criticism).
* **Hetvābhāsa (ID #229)**: The critical identification of the five types of logical fallacies.

## **From Precision to Freedom**

* **Navya-nyāyaḥ (ID #231)**: The advanced analytical logic of **Gaṅgeśa Upādhyāya**, used to eliminate all linguistic ambiguity.
* **Apavarga-mārgaḥ (ID #251)**: The transformative process where **Tattva-jñāna** (True Knowledge) removes **Mithyā-jñāna** (False Knowledge), leading through the removal of defects (**Doṣa**) and suffering (**Duḥkha**) to ultimate **Apavarga** (Liberation).
