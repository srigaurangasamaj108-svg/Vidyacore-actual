## **Skandhatrayam (स्कन्धत्रयम्): The Pillars**

Jyotiṣa is historically divided into three massive branches:

1. **Siddhānta (सिद्धान्तः)**: The pure mathematical calculation of planetary positions and cosmic cycles.
2. **Saṃhitā (संहिता)**: Mundane astrology, natural laws, and collective destiny.
3. **Horā (होरा)**: Predictive and natal astrology focused on individual karma.
4. **Muhūrta (मुहूर्तम्)**: The applied science of selecting auspicious moments for action.

## **Pañcāṅgam (पञ्चाङ्गम्): The Cosmic Clock**

Every point in time is defined by five parameters:
* **Tithi**: The lunar day.
* **Vāra**: The solar weekday.
* **Nakṣatra**: The lunar mansion.
* **Yoga**: The luni-solar angle.
* **Karaṇa**: The half-tithi.

## **Kālamānam (कालमानम्): Rhythms of the Cosmos**

This dimension explores the various clocks of the universe:
* **Saura & Candra Cakra**: The solar and lunar cycles.
* **Ṛtu-cakram**: The six seasons that breathe life into the Earth.
* **Ayanadvayam**: The two solstitial halves of the year.
* **Yuga-cakram**: The vast scales of cosmic time.

## **Vaidika-jyotiṣam (वैदिकज्योतिषम्): The Root**

The original Vedāṅga as described by Sage **Lagadha**. It exists in two primary streams:
1. **Ārca-jyotiṣam**: The Rigvedic tradition.
2. **Yājuṣa-jyotiṣam**: The Yajurvedic tradition.
It serves as the essential tool for determining the correct time for Vedic sacrifices.
