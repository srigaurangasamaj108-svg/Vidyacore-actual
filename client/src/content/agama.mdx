---

**Agama** refers to a massive collection of scriptures that outline the practical methodology of Sanatana Dharma. The word *A-gama* literally means "that which has come down" or "received tradition." If Nigama is the theory, Agama is the **Applied Technology**.

---

## 🏛️ The Engineering of Worship

Agama translates abstract Vedic truths into concrete practices that anyone can follow daily. It governs:
1.  **Temple Architecture**: The physical manifestation of the cosmic order.
2.  **Prana Pratishtha**: The consecration of deities to make God recognizable and accessible.
3.  **Puja Protocols**: Step-by-step physical rituals and yogic practices.

### 🔱 Three Major Branches
Agamas are categorized based on the primary deity they revere:
- **Vaishnava Agamas**: Lord Vishnu (Pancharatra and Vaikhānasa).
- **Shaiva Agamas**: Lord Shiva.
- **Shakta Agamas**: The Divine Mother (often referred to as Tantras).

---

## 🤝 The Complementary Eye

As highlighted in the **CC Adi 2.24**, the Lord is the Absolute Whole described in BOTH Vedas (Nigama) and Agamas. Agama champions the worship of God with recognizable forms and attributes, providing the "Practical Vehicle" to reach the destination shown on the Nigama "Map."

> *"The Personality of Godhead is He who is described as the Absolute Whole in the Vedas, Bhāgavatam, Upaniṣads, Agama-sastras and other transcendental literatures."*
