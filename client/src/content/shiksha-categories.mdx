## **Prātiśākhyas (प्रातिशाख्यप्रकाराः): The Applied Phonetics**

**Prātiśākhyas** are the oldest layer of phonetic manuals in the Vedic tradition. Unlike general phonetic texts, each Prātiśākhya is dedicated to a specific Vedic branch (**Śākhā**), detailing the exact rules for the pronunciation of vowels, consonants, and accents within that particular recension.

## **Ṛṣi-praṇītāḥ Śikṣāḥ (रिषिप्रणीताः शिक्षाः): Theoretical Phonetics**

This category includes the diverse Śikṣā texts composed by ancient Sages (like Pāṇini and Nārada). These works provide the theoretical and universal principles of Sanskrit phonology, applicable across various traditions and often exploring the spiritual significance of sound.
