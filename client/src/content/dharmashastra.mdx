## **Dharmaśāstra (धर्मशास्त्रम्): The Science of Righteousness**

**Dharmaśāstra** is the comprehensive shastric system that governs the ethical, social, and legal life of the individual and the community. It provides the practical framework for **Varṇāśrama Dharma**—the duties related to one's social role and stage of life.

---

### **1. The 16 Saṃskāras (Life-Cycle Rites)**
Dharmaśāstra defines sixteen primary rituals that refine the individual from conception to the final transition:

1. **Garbhādhāna** (Conception)
2. **Puṃsavana** (Fetal protection)
3. **Sīmantonnayana** (Parting the hair/Mother's well-being)
4. **Jātakarma** (Birth rituals)
5. **Nāmakaraṇa** (Naming ceremony)
6. **Niṣkramaṇa** (First outing)
7. **Annaprāśana** (First solid food)
8. **Cūḍākaraṇa** (Tonsure)
9. **Karṇavedha** (Ear piercing)
10. **Vidyārambha** (Beginning of education)
11. **Upanayana** (Sacred thread/Initiation)
12. **Vedārambha** (Beginning of Vedic study)
13. **Keśānta/Ṛtuśuddhi** (First shaving/Coming of age)
14. **Samāvartana** (Completion of education)
15. **Vivāha** (Marriage)
16. **Antyeṣṭi** (Funeral rites)

---

### **2. The 18 Titles of Law (Vyavahāra-padas)**
For the administration of justice, the classical Smṛtis (like *Manu* and *Yājñavalkya*) classify legal disputes into eighteen definitive categories:

1. **Ṛṇādāna** (Recovery of Debts)
2. **Nikṣepa** (Deposits and Pledges)
3. **Asvāmivikraya** (Sale without Ownership)
4. **Sambhūyasamutthāna** (Joint Venture/Concerns)
5. **Dattasyānapakarma** (Resumption of Gifts)
6. **Vetanasyānapakarma** (Non-payment of Wages)
7. **Saṃvidvyatikrama** (Breach of Contract)
8. **Krayavikrayānuśaya** (Rescission of Purchase/Sale)
9. **Svāmipālavivāda** (Disputes between Owners and Herdsmen)
10. **Sīmāvivāda** (Boundary Disputes)
11. **Vākpāruṣya** (Defamation/Abuse)
12. **Daṇḍapāruṣya** (Assault/Physical Injury)
13. **Steya** (Theft)
14. **Sāhasa** (Violence/Robbery)
15. **Strīsaṅgrahaṇa** (Adultery/Crimes against Women)
16. **Strīpuṃdharma** (Duties of Husband and Wife)
17. **Vibhāga** (Partition/Inheritance)
18. **Dyūtasaṃāhvaya** (Gambling and Betting)

### **3. Literary Architecture**
As seen in the sidebar, the Dharmaśāstra tradition is manifest through three primary literary genres:
* **Dharma-sūtrāṇi**: The concise aphoristic foundations (e.g., *Gautama*, *Baudhāyana*).
* **Smṛti-vibhāgaḥ**: The metrical codes (e.g., *Manu*, *Yājñavalkya*, *Parāśara*).
* **Nibandha-granthaḥ**: The great medieval digests and syntheses.
