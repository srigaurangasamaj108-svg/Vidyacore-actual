## **Sāhityika-ādhāra (साहित्याधारः): The Authorities**

The science of prosody is anchored by the **Chandaḥśāstra** of Sage **Piṅgala**. This work is foundational not only for Sanskrit poetry but also for the global history of mathematics, as it contains the earliest known description of a binary system.

## **Vaidika-chandas (वैदिकछन्दांसि): The Rhythm of Revelation**

The Vedic meters are the "Feet" upon which the revelation stands. They are defined by their syllable counts (**Sapta-chandas**) and their unique flexibility:

* **Syllabic Flexibility**: Standard meters can vary. **Nicṛt** (one less), **Bhūrij** (one more), **Svarāṭ** (two more), and **Virāṭ** (two less).
* **Musical Correlation**: Each meter is linked to a specific musical note (**Svara**), connecting Chandas to the Gandharva Veda.

## **Laukika-chandas (लौकिकछन्दांसि): The Poetic Flow**

Classical Sanskrit poetry uses two primary systems:
1. **Akṣara-vṛtta**: Meters based on the count and pattern of syllables.
2. **Mātrā-vṛtta**: Meters based on time units (*Morae*).
3. **Yati-vicāraḥ**: The critical science of the **Caesura** (pause), which dictates the breath and flow of recitation.

## **Metric-logic (छन्दःशास्त्रगणितम्): Binary Architecture**

Chandas is a purely mathematical science:
* **Laghu & Guru**: The binary units (Light/Heavy) of sound.
* **Aṣṭa-gaṇāḥ**: The eight possible three-syllable triplets that form the base of every verse.
* **Combinatorics**: Algorithms like **Prastāra** (expansion) and **Naṣṭam** (reconstruction) to map all possible rhythmic patterns.
