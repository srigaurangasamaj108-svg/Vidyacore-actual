# Nigama: The Mature Fruit 🌿🏛️

### 🕉️ Opening Mantra
> **निगमकल्पतरोर्गलितं फलं शुकमुखादमृतद्रवसंयुतम् ।**  
> **पिबत भागवतं रसमालयं मुहुरहो रसिका भुवि भावुका: ॥ ३ ॥**  
>
> *nigama-kalpa-taror galitaṁ phalaṁ śuka-mukhād amṛta-drava-saṁyutam*  
> *pibata bhāgavataṁ rasam ālayam muhur aho rasikā bhuvi bhāvukāḥ*
>
> **[Srimad Bhagavatam 1.1.3](https://vedabase.io/en/library/sb/1/1/3/)**

---

**Nigama** is traditionally synonymous with the **Vedas**—the oldest and most authoritative scriptures of Sanatana Dharma. The word *Ni-gama* literally points to "that which is fixed, arranged, or conclusive." It represents the "Theory" and the "Pure Science" of the Absolute Truth.

---

## 🌪️ The Desire Tree of Knowledge

As described in the opening mantra (**SB 1.1.3**), the Nigama is compared to a **Kalpataru** (Desire Tree). All branches of Vedic knowledge flow from this tree, and the Srimad Bhagavatam is considered its fully matured, nectarean fruit.

### 🏛️ The Character of Nigama
- **Apauruṣeya Source**: The Vedas are beginningless sound. Ancient sages (*Rishis*) did not "write" them; they "heard" or directly realized them in deep states of meditation.
- **Philosophical Map**: Nigama provides the vast, macro-philosophical map of the universe, explaining *what* the ultimate truth is.

---

## ⚙️ The Goal of Nigama

The ultimate purpose of Nigama is to provide the intellectual and spiritual foundation for the seeker. It provides the **Pure Science** that ensures all spiritual practice remains anchored in absolute truth.

> *"O expert and thoughtful men, relish Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, the mature fruit of the desire tree of Vedic literatures."*
