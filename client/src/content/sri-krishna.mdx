# Sri Krishna: The Absolute Source 🌿🏛️

### 🕉️ Opening Sutra
> **सर्वस्य चाहं हृदि सन्निविष्टो**  
> **मत्तः स्मृतिर्ज्ञानमपोहनं च ।**  
> **वेदैश्च सर्वैर्हमेव वेद्यो**  
> **वेदान्तकृद्वेदविदेव चाहम् ॥ १५ ॥**  
>
> *sarvasya cāhaṁ hṛdi sanniviṣṭो*  
> *mattaḥ smṛtir jñānam apohanaṁ ca*  
> *vedaiś ca sarvair aham eva vedyo*  
> *vedānta-kṛd veda-vid eva cāham*  
>
> **[Bhagavad Gita 15.15](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/15/15/)**

---

Śrī Kṛṣṇa is established as the absolute source of all Vedic literature and the ultimate Personality of Godhead through direct statements in the Vedas and the Bhagavad Gita. While mundane literature is written by conditioned humans, the Vedas are **apauruṣeya** (not of human origin) and emanate directly from the breathing of the Supreme Lord.

---

## 📚 Source of All Vedic Literature

Scriptures explicitly state that the Vedas are not compiled by human intellect but are the divine breath and instructions of Kṛṣṇa:

### 💨 The Divine Breath
The **Brihadaranyaka Upanishad (2.4.10)** states that the Rig, Yajur, Sama, and Atharva Vedas are all the breathing of the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

### 🗣️ The Speaker of the Gita
As declared in the opening sutra above (**BG 15.15**), Kṛṣṇa explicitly clarifies His role as the compiler and the ultimate goal of all Vedic study.

### 💖 The Original Teacher
In the **[Bhagavata Purana (1.1.1)](https://vedabase.io/en/library/sb/1/1/1/)**, it is described that the Supreme Lord originally imparted the Vedic knowledge into the heart of Brahma, the first created living being, who then disseminated it.

### ✍️ As Vyāsadeva
To make the vast Vedic knowledge accessible to the people of the current age (Kali Yuga), Kṛṣṇa descended as the literary incarnation, **Sage Vyāsadeva**, to compile and divide the single Veda into four and write the Mahabharata and the Puranas.

---

## 👑 The Absolute Personality of Godhead

In Vedic theology, the Absolute Truth is realized in three progressive features: **Brahman** (formless light), **Paramātmā** (Supersoul), and **Bhagavān** (Supreme Person). Kṛṣṇa is Bhagavān, the highest realization.

- **Possessor of All Opulences**: Parāśara Muni defines Bhagavān as one who possesses infinite wealth, strength, fame, beauty, knowledge, and renunciation.
- **Source of Brahman**: Kṛṣṇa clarifies in **[Gita (14.27)](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/14/27/)** that He is the very source and basis of the impersonal Brahman (*brahmaṇo hi pratiṣṭhāham*).
- **The Cause of All Causes**: The **Brahma Samhita (5.1)** confirms: *"īśvaraḥ paramaḥ kṛṣṇaḥ sac-cid-ānanda-vigrahaḥ anādir ādir govindaḥ sarva-kāraṇa-kāraṇam."*

---

## ☸️ The Ultimate Purpose of the Vedas

Kṛṣṇa explains that the ultimate purpose of all complex Vedic rituals is simply to surrender to Him in love:

1. **Gradual Elevation**: The Vedas cater to different modes of nature, elevating seekers from religiosity to renunciation.
2. **The Ultimate Conclusion**: In **[Bhagavad Gita (18.66)](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/18/66/)**, Kṛṣṇa delivers the final word:
> *"Abandon all varieties of religion and just surrender unto Me. I shall deliver you from all sinful reactions. Do not fear."*
