## **Sāhityika-ādhāra (साहित्यिकआधारः): The Texts**

The Nirukta tradition is anchored by two layers of literature:

1. **Nighaṇṭu (निघण्टुः)**: The raw glossary of 1,760 Vedic words.
2. **Niruktam (निरुक्तम्)**: Sage Yāska's Processing Engine—the etymological explanation of how those words function.

## **Catvāri Padajātāni (चत्वारि पदजातानि): The Linguistic Soul**

1. **Nāma** (Noun): Expresses static being or an entity.
2. **Ākhyāta** (Verb): Expresses becoming or an active process. In Nirukta, the verb is the primary reality.
3. **Upasarga** (Prefix): Modifiers that qualify the meaning of nouns or verbs.
4. **Nipāta** (Particle): Indeclinable words used for comparison or filling meter.

## **Ṣaḍ Bhāva-vikārāḥ (षड् भावविकाराः): The Metaphysical Bridge**

Nirukta connects linguistics to the laws of nature through the six stages of becoming:
1. **Jāyate** (Is born).
2. **Asti** (Exists).
3. **Vipariṇamate** (Modifies).
4. **Vardhate** (Grows).
5. **Apakṣīyate** (Decays).
6. **Vinaśyati** (Perishes).

## **Nirvacana-rītiḥ (निर्वचनरीतिः): Hermeneutic Insight**

* **Pratyakṣa-vṛtti**: Obvious derivation.
* **Parokṣa-vṛtti**: Obscured root.
* **Atiparokṣa-vṛtti**: Hidden or riddle-like derivation that reveals spiritual truth.

## **Nirukta-darśanam (निरुक्तदर्शनम्): Dhātujatvam**

The central pillar of Nirukta is **Dhātujatvam**—the realization that every object in the universe is named based on what it *does*. A word is a functional truth.
